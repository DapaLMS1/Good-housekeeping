Maintaining Good Housekeeping Practices

Maintaining **good housekeeping practices** is fundamental to workplace safety, especially for a **Dental Assistant**. It forms the bedrock of a safe and efficient environment, preventing accidents and promoting overall well-being.

Identifying Risks and Implementing Controls

Good housekeeping involves actively **identifying risks** associated with poor hygiene and implementing effective **controls**. This includes recognizing potential hazards like contaminated surfaces, cluttered pathways, or improperly stored equipment that could compromise patient and staff safety.

Effective Procedures for Hazard Control

**Effective procedures** help control or eliminate hazards, such as **slip and trip risks**, through **regular cleaning and maintenance**. This proactive approach ensures that the dental practice remains a safe and sterile environment, protecting everyone within it.
